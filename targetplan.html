Part F: Plan Completeness & Next Steps

20. Potential Missing Elements & Further Discovery

While this plan incorporates significant detail, achieving a "perfect" plan requires ongoing validation and refinement through direct engagement. Key areas potentially missing or needing deeper discovery include:

  • Specific Quantified Metrics: While ROI levers are identified, the plan needs specific, agreed-upon target metrics (e.g., "Reduce frontline TTH by 25% within 12 months of rollout, saving an estimated $X million annually"). This requires collaborative work with Target using their baseline data.
  • Detailed Decision Criteria: What specific technical, functional, and commercial criteria will Target use to formally evaluate Eightfold against competitors (including Workday)? Understanding their weighting is crucial.
  • Procurement & Legal Process ("Paper Process"): What are Target's exact steps, timelines, and key internal contacts for reviewing and approving MSAs, DPAs, SOWs, and issuing POs? Are there standard terms they typically push for?
  • Target-Side Resource Commitment: What specific personnel (names, roles, time commitment) will Target need to dedicate for a successful implementation (Project Manager, HR Tech Lead, Functional SMEs, Change Lead, Trainers)?
  • Competitor Weaknesses (Target Context): Explicitly documenting *why* Workday, Avature, SeekOut, etc., are insufficient *for Target's specific scale and strategic goals*, based on discovery findings.
  • Detailed Change Management Plan: While the need is identified, a detailed plan outlining communication strategy, training approach (especially for frontline), resistance management, and success measurement needs co-development.

These elements typically emerge and are refined during the later stages of the sales cycle (Discovery, Prove, Validate) as outlined in the Engagement Plan (Section 15).
